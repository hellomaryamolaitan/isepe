Step into the story

An afternoon of stories, conversations, and culture

Join us as we celebrate creativity, where laughter meets reflection, and every question opens a doorway to meaning. The launch of ÌSÈPÉ isn’t just about a book — it’s about culture, memory, and the stories that live within each of us. Come ready to listen, share, and be inspired.

Venue: Polygon HQ, 5, Idowu Taylor, VI, Lagos

Date: September 20, 2025

Time: 1pm - 5pm (no African time)

YUSUF ÀLÀBÍ BALÓGUN

Author - ÌSÈPÉ

Yusuf Alàbí Balógun (Àremo Gemini) is a multifaceted artist - poet, storyteller, TEDx speaker, art specialist, with a core focus in Yorubá arts and culture.

Yusuf Alàbí Balógun is the voice behind Segílolá Arómiré Ogidán and now, ÌSÈPÉ. As an indie storyteller of Yorùbá literature, he doesn’t just write stories — Yusuf Alabi Balogun preserves culture, asks hard questions, and opens doors to conversations about family, tradition, and survival in today’s world.

ỌLÁYÍNKÁ ÀRÌNPÉ Ẹ̀KÚNRẸ́RẸ́

Host

Ọláyínká Àrìnpé Ẹ̀kúnrẹ́rẹ́ is a lover and student of the Yorùbá Culture. She is a cultural facilitator, deeply committed to the preservation and promotion of Yorùbá cultural heritage.

Her works bridge language, literature, and the performing arts. With the belief that literature is the vehicle that moves language forward, Ọláyínká has a vested interest in high-quality Yorùbá literary work. She is also dedicated to reigniting the love of Yorùbá Literature in young lovers and L2 Learners by commiting to writing relatable flash stories that can help start their journey of literary appreciation.

She is also a poet, researcher, Akéwì and a host.

MARYAM MOJÍSỌ́LÁ ỌLÁÌTÁN

Fireside Chat Moderator

Maryam Ọláìtán spends her days crafting websites and her nights chasing colours — now stepping boldly into her creative journey — because one career clearly isn’t enough excitement. At this book launch, she’s not only moderating, she’s here to guide the conversations with warmth, making sure the only thing silent in the room is the books themselves.
